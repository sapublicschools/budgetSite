The next core area of the budget is appropriations - how the money is spent. As we discussed on the last page, the vast majority of the budget comes from Local Tax Levy and State Aid. Now we will see how it is allocated.
2017-2018 Appropriations

Like one would expect from a school district, the majority of our funding is spent on instruction. This includes teacher salaries, instructional materials, and other miscellaneous costs related to daily educational necessities.

For legal reasons, Special Education costs are listed separately. A vast minority of our students require Special Education services, but the necessary extra services are costly.

Out-of-District Placements

Out-of-District Tuition and Transportation is an area of our budget that has been growing particularly quickly. When a student enters our district who requires services (typically some sort of special education) that we cannot provide on-site, we are legally obligated to send the student to a school that provides those services - regardless of cost. Unfortunately, the state provides very little assistance for this type of payment.

This area of the budget has grown by nearly $1,000,000 in the last four years. This means that approximately 20 students, or 1.67% of our student body, account for nearly 20% of our budget.

This fast growth has been an area of concern, so we have taken steps to attempt to mitigate its effect on our budget. This year, we implemented a Life Skills program within our district. The investment allows us to keep students within their home district instead of paying for additional out-of-district placements. In the future, we hope to offer the programs to children in need from other districts.
