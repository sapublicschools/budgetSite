Budget Portal

2017-2018
Welcome! The purpose of this website is to provide an approachable and thorough introduction to the budget of South Amboy School District, a K-12 public school system that serves approximately 1200 students in pre-kindergarten through 12th grade. Official information from the Business Office can be found here, but this website serves as an introduction for those who are not familiar with school budgets and how they affect your local property tax bill.

Revenues

2017-2018
A budget is broken down into two sections: revenue and appropriations. We will start by describing each of these two sections in detail.
2017-2018 Revenues Chart

The vast majority of our funding comes from two sources: State Aid and Local Tax Levy.

"Other" contains relatively small amounts of funding from various sources. If you're interested in finding out exactly what falls in this category and a more detailed view of revenues, you can find out here.

2017-2018 Revenues Table

This is the same information presented in the previous chart, but compares it to last year. Notice that State Aid remained constant, while the Local Tax Levy increased by 2%. This is the section of the budget that is funded by annual property taxes. You may have heard of a "Tax Cap" - this refers to the law in New Jersey which states that local government entitites like school districts can only increase the annual tax levy by 2%.

However, notice that it is not a 2% increase on the district budget but rather a 2% increase on one section of the budget. Even with this 2% increase, you can see that our budget is slightly lower than last year's because of decreases in other sources of state and federal funding. For a more detailed view, click here.
